Topic: Global Economic Indicators

Economic indicators are statistics that provide insights into a country's economic performance. Inflation rates, interest rates, and fiscal policies influence macroeconomic stability. Gross Domestic Product (GDP) measures the total value of goods and services produced. Leading, lagging, and coincident indicators help economists predict trends and guide policy. Unemployment rate indicates the percentage of the labor force that is jobless and seeking work. Trade balances show the difference between exports and imports of goods and services. Consumer confidence reflects household sentiment about the economy and spending willingness. Global economic indicators are monitored by investors, governments, and central banks for decision making.
